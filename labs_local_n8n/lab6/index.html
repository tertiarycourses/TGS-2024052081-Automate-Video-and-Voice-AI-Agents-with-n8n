How it works

  • Local engine — your script is converted to phonemes, phonemes to visemes (mouth shapes), and the timeline is stretched onto the real audio. Live loudness modulates jaw drop, so pauses actually close the mouth. Nothing leaves your machine.
  • Export — Browser TTS audio can't be captured by the page, so exporting needs Piper, ElevenLabs, OpenAI, or an uploaded audio file.
  • Render photoreal — hands the shot to MuseTalk, a neural model that generates real mouth pixels on your own machine (~1 min). This is the one to use for anything you'd actually show someone.
  • HeyGen — renders in the cloud and costs credits, but it's the only engine that also moves the head and blinks.
